About Me

I am a rising junior at USC who is interested in machine learning research for holistic societal applications. As a first-generation college student from a low-income background, I am passionate about using sustainable technology to empower and support families burdened by trauma, poverty, and pollution. My first exposure to AI research was during my freshman year of college where I worked with my university's computer media lab to research music API models for patients with cognitive disabilites. Now, I am interning at my university's signal processing lab and Cornell University to program and experiment with machine learning algorithms for a variety of applications.

The creAItivity AI Ethics Lab provides students the opportunity to deliver a project focused on technology and social justice. As a fellow, I worked with my cohort to research and design a circuit that could determine whether an individual was angry based on the volume of their voice.

Developed as a final project for my distributed systems class, this loT system creates a three-way connection between the Raspberry Pi and two RESTFUL APIs combined with machine learning to predict music genre using volume sensor data and return song reccomendations based on the predicted genre.

The Single Molecule Biophysics Group at the University of Southern California offers a summer research program in nanoengineering exploration. My responsibilities included protein extraction and visualizing protein concentrations in patients with muscular dystrophy using imaging technology.

In conjunction with Google, the Center Computational Media Intelligence at the University of Southern California organized a 5-month project to explore human-centered AI technology. My responsibility included analyzing various music APIs to develop a final presentation on holistic media applications.

The Signal Analysis and Interpretation Laboratory at the University of Southern California collects a compilation of environmental and biometric data to monitor the emotional health of low-income families. My resposnibilites include programming and experiment with models to process data.
