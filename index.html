About Me

I am an Electrical and Computer Engineering student passionate about sustainability and facilitating positive interactions between the environment and emerging technologies. Even as technology continues to evolve, sustainable development is vital to the future of biodiversity and culture preservation. As an engineer with prior research experience, my skills include programming primarily in C/C++ and hardware design. In addition, I have experience using CAD software such as Autodesk and NX, as well as creating graphic designs.

My interest in engineering sparked when I created my first robot in the eighth grade, and since then I have developed an old habit of tinkering and breaking apart old electronics for spare parts. I later went on to attend an engineering-focused high school where I participated in clubs such as Girls Build LA, USC's Mathematics Engineering Science Achievement (MESA), and Solar Boat. In the summer of my junior year in high school, I participated in a research program at USC to study the causes of muscular dystrophy. This opportunity helped me realize how much I enjoy applying my knowledge through hands-on experiments to solve real-world problems. As a first-generation college student, my goal is to help students from underprivileged communities obtain a secondary education.
